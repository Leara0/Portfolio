About Me

I bring a unique combination of scientific rigor, healthcare insight, and technical problem-solving to software development. With a background in biology from Cornell University and a Master's in Nursing, I've spent years thinking systematically about complex problems - from understanding biological systems to managing patient care.

My career has taken me across the country and around the world, developing adaptability and cross-cultural communication skills along the way. After exploring healthcare and entrepreneurial ventures, I discovered my passion for programming through TrueCoders' full-stack development bootcamp. What excites me most about software development is the same thing that drew me to science originally: the challenge of understanding complex systems and finding elegant solutions to difficult problems.

I'm driven by curiosity and continuous learning. Currently, I volunteer as a program coordinator, where I manage logistics, solve scheduling challenges, and coordinate teams - skills that translate directly to project management and collaborative development work. I also volunteer with healthcare advocacy organizations, keeping me connected to the intersection of technology and meaningful impact.

I'm eager to bring my analytical mindset, healthcare domain knowledge, and fresh development skills to a team that values diverse perspectives and problem-solving ability.
